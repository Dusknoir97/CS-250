This repository includes my Sprint Review and Retrospective from the SNHU Travel project. This project focused on working through different roles on a Scrum team.

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by  understanding what the customer wants the program to accomplish and then breaking those ideas into smaller features that can be developed. User stories helped make requirements easier to understand because they focused on what the user needed rather than technical details. 

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach developing programs by first planning how the application should work and then building features one at a time instead of trying to complete everything at once. I learned how useful Agile processes can be, especially when requirements change. I hope to incorporate sprint planning and feedback to keep projects moving forward. The idea of breaking larger problems into smaller ones makes development feel more manageable.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member in software development means communicating and collarborating to support the success of the project. Team members should be willing to accept feedback and stay accountable for completing their work. Communication between roles such as the Product Owner, Developer, Tester, and Scrum Master is important because everyone depends on one another to complete the project successfully.
